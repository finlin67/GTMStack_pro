I build sales enablement programs that combine content libraries, playbooks, and tools aligned to sales process and revenue outcomes. This isn't enablement for its own sake—it's sales enablement tied to revenue.

## Route Map

### 1. Map Sales Process & Enablement Needs

Start with sales process mapping—prospecting, discovery, demo, proposal, close. Identify enablement needs at each stage and define success metrics. Build enablement frameworks that align to sales process and buyer journey. This is strategic enablement architecture, not just content.

### 2. Build Content Libraries & Playbooks

Develop content libraries aligned to sales process and buyer journey—case studies, demos, proposals, battlecards. Create sales playbooks that enable systematic sales execution. Build content management systems that enable easy access and usage. This is systematic enablement, not ad-hoc content.

### 3. Instrument Enablement Tools & Integration

Set up enablement platforms—Seismic, Highspot, Pathfactory—for content management and distribution. Integrate with CRM (Salesforce) and marketing automation for unified enablement. Build enablement analytics that track usage and impact. This is enablement infrastructure, not just tools.

### 4. Launch Enablement Programs

Launch enablement programs aligned to sales process and buyer journey. Implement content libraries, playbooks, and tools. Train sales team on enablement usage and best practices. This is enablement execution, not just planning.

### 5. Measure & Optimize Performance

Track enablement metrics—content usage, sales cycle, win rates, pipeline contribution. Build dashboards that show enablement performance by program and revenue outcome. Optimize content and playbooks based on performance data. This is enablement optimization tied to revenue.

## FAQs

**Q: How do you measure sales enablement ROI in B2B when impact is on sales?**

A: I build enablement measurement frameworks that track sales efficiency—content usage, sales cycle, win rates—and revenue impact—pipeline contribution, deal velocity, ACV. I connect enablement metrics to revenue outcomes through CRM and analytics. I also track enablement performance by sales stage and buyer journey. The goal is directional clarity that shows enablement is accelerating deals and improving win rates. I've built systems that show enablement impact on sales within 90–180 days.

**Q: What's the difference between sales enablement and marketing content?**

A: Marketing content is buyer-focused—blog posts, ebooks, webinars—that educates and converts buyers. Sales enablement is sales-focused—case studies, demos, proposals—that supports sales conversations and accelerates deals. I build enablement programs that include marketing content but also include sales-specific content, playbooks, and tools. The goal is enablement that drives revenue, not just content.

**Q: How do you build sales enablement programs that scale?**

A: I build enablement frameworks with content libraries, playbooks, and tool integration. I integrate enablement with marketing, sales, and product so enablement becomes systematic, not ad-hoc. I also use enablement platforms and analytics to scale content and measurement. The goal is systematic enablement, not manual content management. I've built programs that scale enablement across sales teams and buyer journeys.
